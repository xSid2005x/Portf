Project Plantae Irrigation

Our project minimises manual labour, create solutions to reduce infestation of weeds/pests in crops and predicts harsh weather conditions. Driving Question: How can we leverage innovative technology to develop an automated plant irrigation system that enhances crop productivity, addresses climate challenges, and minimizes the need for manual labor? Project Outline: In response to the adverse effects of climate change on agriculture, I embarked on creating a sustainable solution to empower farmers and increase crop productivity while reducing manual labor. The key focus areas were: Climate-Resilient Irrigation: Developing an automated plant irrigation system using M5 Stack and sensors capable of adjusting watering schedules based on real-time weather data. This technology aims to optimize water usage, ensuring crops receive the right amount of water at the appropriate times. Smart Pest and Disease Detection: Implementing advanced sensors to detect early signs of pests and fungi infestation, allowing timely intervention and minimizing crop damage. This proactive approach reduces the need for extensive manual labor in pest management. Sustainable Farming Practices: By combining technology, climate-resilient irrigation, and precision agriculture, the project promotes sustainable farming practices. By conserving water, reducing manual labor, and improving crop health, it contributes to greater agricultural efficiency and resilience. Through this innovative project, I aimed to empower farmers with an automated plant irrigation system that optimizes crop growth, adapts to changing climatic conditions, and reduces reliance on manual labor. The integration of M5 Stack and sensor technologies demonstrates the potential of using cutting-edge tools to address contemporary agricultural challenges and foster sustainable food production.
