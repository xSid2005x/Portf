Data-driven Mushroom Cultivation

In this data-driven project, our team aimed to revolutionize oyster mushroom cultivation using cutting-edge technology. Leveraging Arduino and sensors, we meticulously monitored and controlled environmental factors, including temperature, humidity, and light, to optimize growth conditions. As a dedicated coder and data analyzer, my role was pivotal in developing the Arduino code for real-time data collection. I also implemented a secure database to store and analyze sensor readings, providing valuable insights into the growth process. Throughout the project, we fine-tuned temperature control mechanisms and experimented with various approaches to achieve the desired results. Our efforts paid off, with one mushroom substrate reaching an impressive 95% whiteness, signifying successful growth and validating the effectiveness of our data-driven approach. The project showcased our expertise in utilizing technology to drive sustainable agricultural practices. By harnessing data for informed decision-making, we achieved efficient oyster mushroom cultivation and opened doors to future advancements in the field. Overall, this project has been a rewarding experience, allowing me to showcase my coding and data analysis skills in a practical and impactful setting. It has further solidified my passion for data-driven solutions and their potential to transform traditional practices into more sustainable and efficient methods.
